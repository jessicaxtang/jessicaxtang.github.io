    • Rehabilitation Exercise Quality Assessment and Feedback Generation Using Large Language Models with Prompt Engineering.
      Jessica Tang, Ali Abedi, Tracey JF Colella, Shehroz Khan. ARIAL workshop at IJCAI 2025. [Code]
      We designed exercise-specific feature extraction methods to distill key movement quality indicators from skeletal joint data. Combined with carefully optimized prompts, these features enable large language models to both assess exercise quality and give personalized, actionable feedback, highlighting their potential to make virtual rehabilitation more accurate, adaptive, and patient-friendly.
    • Spatio-temporal Multimodal Wildfire Spread Prediction: Exploring Vision Transformers and Lightweight Models.
      Jessica Tang, Hibah Hussain, Natasha Yang (equal contribution). ECE324 Final Project 2025.
      We developed FireNet, a vision transformer-based model that predicts wildfire spread using satellite imagery and meteorological data. Our model outperforms traditional CNNs in accuracy and efficiency, demonstrating the potential of transformers in environmental monitoring applications.

    • Disputations on Compatibilism and the No Choice Principle.
      Jessica Tang. PHL244 Human Nature, 2024.
      An exploration of Van Inwagen's "No Choice Principle" and the debate over compatibilism, examining whether free will can coexist with determinism and presenting objections that challenge his incompatibilist conclusion.
    • Does Mass Media Enable Genocide?
      Jessica Tang. Harvard College Undergraduate Research Association, 2020.
      This piece explores how mass media can fuel violence, using the Rwandan Genocide as an example. It shows how radio propaganda encouraged conformity and escalated conflict, revealing the powerful role media can play in enabling atrocities.

    • What's Your Groove? Beat Tracking in Music Signal Processing.
      Jessica Tang. ECE355 Independent Study Module, 2024.
      This paper examines beat tracking in music, focusing on tempo and beat detection using Music Information Retrieval (MIR) techniques. It highlights how musical features like pitch, intensity, and timbre map to signal representations and emphasizes energy-based beat detection using the short-time Fourier transform (STFT). Advanced beat tracking methods and their applications in the music industry are also discussed.
